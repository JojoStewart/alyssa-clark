Research

Nonprofit-Corporate Impact Measurement

Ashoka · Washington, DC · 2019

Overview

Independently scoped and managed a research project to develop an impact measurement framework for nonprofit-corporate partnerships at Ashoka, the world's largest network of social entrepreneurs. The project was delivered to the corporate partnerships team as both a written report and presentation.

Context

Ashoka's corporate alliances programme connects social entrepreneurs with corporations pursuing social impact. Measuring the impact of these partnerships — which span different sectors, geographies, and definitions of success — required a framework that could accommodate complexity while remaining practical for the partnerships team.

Approach

  • Independently scoped the research question, methodology, and deliverables
  • Reviewed existing literature on partnership impact measurement
  • Developed a framework tailored to the unique dynamics of nonprofit-corporate collaboration
  • Delivered findings as a written report and presentation to the corporate partnerships team

Outcomes

  • Delivered a practical impact measurement framework adopted by the corporate partnerships team
  • Provided a foundation for more consistent impact reporting across Ashoka's corporate alliances
